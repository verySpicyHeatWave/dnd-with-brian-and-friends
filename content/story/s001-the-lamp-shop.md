## Story

### The Lute & Lyre

The story starts in the mid-sized trade town of [Norville](../../setting/estharia/norville). It's a wet, cold, stinky, rainy spring day, some time between 2 and 4 pm, maybe 3 pm, who's to say, live your own truth or whatever. Four adventurers are coincidentally drawn to Norville for one reason or another.

[Elowen Giggleleaf](../main-characters/elowen-giggleleaf), the mysterious druid (NOT cleric, thank you very much) was drawn to Norville by the wind, which told her that a beautiful white horse had an injury and needed her help. She found the horse behind the local tavern called [The Lute & Lyre](../../setting/estharia/norville#the-lute--lyre), lying in a stable, injured. Drawing upon her earthly magic, she healed the horse, who gave her a nod and a wink as if to say, "thanks bro, good lookin' out".

[Vesper Treadway](../../main-characters/vesper-treadway) arrived at 3:00 pm as is her own truth and entered the Lute & Lyre. She's been here before, and she'll be back again. She begins to set up to perform, but has arrived a bit early. The tavern is dead, save for a few poor souls already drunk. The coffers are clearly running dry among the patrons here. Even still, the day is early, and she hopes that later on, more people start filing in.

[Aelyra Caladwen](../../characters/main-characters/aelyra-caladwen) and [Syren Ashryver](../../characters/main-characters/syren-ashryver) arrive shortly after Vesper, with the specific intent of Aelyra performing. Aelyra immediately takes note of the stage AND the person on it and immediately thinks, "Oh, her and I could play together, cool!" Aelyra approaches Vesper, who is preparing the space for her performance, and says, "Hey, I see you got an instrument. Yeah. I also have an instrument. Where are you gonna play that instrument? I was gonna do a little ditty." She gestures towareds Syren. "This is my friend. She doesn't have instruments, but she's cool. She's here... She's here for the vibes, okay? And I need money, too."

Syren injerjects on Aelyra's behalf while Aelyra tunes her lyre (a.k.a. baby harp). She invoked Aelyra's status as high nobility of a neighboring country, but Vesper is unimpressed by the credentials. Aelyra begins playing a beautiful quiet ballad-type of song, invoking tears in the eyes of the drunk men all around. Vesper watches, largely unimpressed though silently acknowledging some amount of talent there, straps on her Lute and begins playing Bon Jovi's "Livin' on a Prayer", which also invokes tears. Vesper agrees to allow Aelyra to play as backup, but must follow Vesper's lead. Aelyra agrees, reluctantly.

The characters spend the next few hours "getting hammer drunk" and waiting for more patrons to file in before performing. Hours later, when the tavern finally appears to be sufficiently populated, the musicians take the stage and begin playing what may or may not have been Bob Marley's "Jammin'", and the patrons love it. They're eating it up.

Mere moments into the performance, a man bursts through the tavern door in a panic, shouting for help in a panic. Vesper vaguely recognizes the man as the owner of the [Lamp Emporium](../../setting/estharia/norville#findlays-lamp-emporium), and she and Aelyra stop playing to listen to what he has to say. He notices that he has the attention of the people nearby and continues, rubbing his cheeks with his palms nervously:

> I've spilled tea all over my demonic contracts at the at the lamp shop and now the demon and all the imps that I had serving me are freed and I don't know what to do. They rebelled and kicked me out of the lamp emporium. I can't get back in. They're running the place, Oh, I need to get back in there, I have important blueprints and my spellbook, they have everything. [Belvedere](../../npcs/demons/belvedere), my demon servant, took my finger and is using it to bind a ward that keeps me from being able to enter! Are any of you adventureres that can help me? I can offer many rewards!

 Aelyra immediately agrees to help, and as a result of that, Syren also agrees to help, but only to help Aelyra and keep her out of trouble. Elowen, who has been silently observing from the corner, emerges and also agrees to assist, insisting that she wants to ensure that nobody gets hurt. Vesper expresses some reluctance, and asks, "Well, what's in it for me?" 

 Findlay lays out his terms. The Imps must be dealt with, and for every imp tail brought to him, he will pay four gold. For one of Belvedere's horns, he will also pay, and he will also pay if Belvedere is demonstrably killed or banished from the natural plane. He will pay for the return of his finger, for the breaking of the ward, for the discovery and return of his spellbook, and for a blueprint for a special lamp that he is working on. Elowen asks, "Can I have a lamp?" to which Findlay agrees unconditionally. Vesper decides to join, but asks Findlay to watch her lute, to which he also agrees.

 ### Breaking into The Lamp Emporium

 The players follow Findlay back to the Lamp Emporium. Findlay points to a mark on the ground and says, "I can't get any closer than that to the building." The players test the ward, ensuring that they are not also kept out by the magic. They are not, and so Vesper approaches the primary entrance which has a poorly written and heavily mispelled sign that says, "Closed. Reopening under new management. -B". She tries the door, but finds it locked, and so she produces her thieves kit and picks the lock, which is a little more complex than she's accustomed to, but she gets it unlocked without getting anyone's attention and takes a peek inside to scope out the situation.

 Peering through the door, Vesper can see three imps on the floor of a well-stocked lamp emporium show room. Two of them--one, a skunk-like imp with two parallel stripes running along its back, and the other a small gray demon with enormous pointed ears and a thin tail with a sharp barb at the end of it--are painting a new sign for the lamp emporium. The third, a slightly bigger but still relatively small green creature, paced back and forth with a club, overseeing the sign, nodding with approval at the mispelled words and malformed letters.

Vesper returns to the party to tell them what she saw, and she suggests a silent approach. Syren expresses that she feels confident that she would walk in and kill the imps with no need for secrecy, stating that she is "really powerful". Aelyra suggests that, perhaps they don't kill the cute little guys, but instead, perhaps they could persuade them, and she even suggests using some of her magic to do it. Vesper seems to like that plan, suggesting that they try to simply buy a lamp, or ask for a tour of the lamp emporium. "And then kill them?" Syren suggests, and Aelyra says, "No no no... I think that these imps deserve to live." They start to think about Findlay and his motives, starting to doubt whether they can trust someone who enslaved these creatures, and consider helping the imps instead.

After a discussion about what to do with the imps and Findlay, the party decides to side with the imps and to secure their freedom fully, finding the enslavement of the small infernal creatures to be morally abhorrent, despite their impish nature. The plot consists of simply entering the Lamp Emporium and offering to help Belvedere secure the Lamp Emporium and to help keep Findlay at bay, for a price. Syren offers several times to kill Findlay, to the objections of the rest of the group. Aelyra wants to ease into the situation with a little bit of magical assistance, so she plans to discretely cast Charm Person on the bigger green imp prior to entering the room.

And so they do that. Aelyra very quietly opens the door, reaches her hand inside, and casts Charm person towards the green imp. The spell binds successfully, but the imps all notice the spell being cast from across the room. The larger imp takes no action, being under Aelyra's spell, but the other two imps take action immediately. They drop their paintbrushes and one of the imps--a small, grey one--opens his mouth and breathes out enormous plumes of smoke, filling the room. Aelyrca shouts through the smoke and fog at the imp under her spell, telling him they just want to talk, and he calms his fellow imps down and asks what she wants. Aelyra explains the proposition to the imp, named [Biff](../../npcs/imps/biff), who quietly expresses disdain towards Belvedere and says, "You want to help us? I got someone else you should talk to, come with me." Aelyra calls the rest of her party in and they follow the imp up the stairs.

The second floor is a workshop, with many various lamps in progress, a kiln, and some imps making lamp s there. A small bat-like imp is also working, and that is who Biff takes them to. He says, "[Max](../../npcs/imps/max), these people want to talk to Belvedere", to which Max replies, "Oh... Fuck Belvedere. What do you want with him?" Max reveals that Belvedere is powerful, and wants to crush all of Norville, but the Imps just want to return to their native plane and want no part in Belvedere's overarching evil plan, but when Findlay nulled their contracts, Belvedere immediately bound the impish contracts under his control. The players are reluctant, and ask for a reward to help them, to which Max says that they have no money, but they can have whatever is in the lamp emporium that they want. Vesper asks for their tails in exchange instead, and Max immediately agrees to cut his tail off and says that it's a small price to pay, though he also tells the players that not all of the imps are on board with overthrowing Belvedere.

While this discussion is happening, Syren and Vesper look around to see which imps in the room are in agreement and realize that one of the imps--a small, adorable pink imp named [Yuck](../../npcs/imps/yuck)--is trying to sneak up the stairs to the next floor away from them, and they both sprint after him to bring him back. Syren trips and falls, still feeling the liquor in her belly. Vesper leaps over the prone Syren and grabs the imp by the scruff of the neck, pulling it back into the room with the rest of them. The pink imp begins yelling about how Belvedere will not let Max or the elves get away with their plot against him, and that he's powerful.

Syren, still on the floor, says, "I have a lot of power."

Vesper begins interrogating the pink imp, wanting to know Belvedere's weaknesses and how to find and defeat him, but Yuck is defiant and spits acid at her. Vesper, frustrated with the stubbornness, cuts off the imp's tail and wraps it around her neck like a scarf. After a lot of fruitless (but very spit-ful) back and forth between Yuck and Vesper, Syren steps in and begins taking fingers. Yuck caves and tells the players that Belvedere _loves_ impish bon-bons. After getting the information from Yuck, Syren slits Yuck's throat, killing him.
