## Highlights

- The characters all meet in the Norville tavern, The Lute & Lyre, and prepare for a performance.
- During Vesper and Aelyra's performance, a man bursts through the door pleading for help
- He is the owner of the Lamp Emporium, and tells everyone that he spilled tea on his demonic contracts that bound a demon and several imps to his servitude, which voided the contracts and freed the demons and imps. He's lost controls of his shop and needs help breaking the ward, killing the demon and the imps, and getting back into his shop and home.
- The characters agree to help!
- They head to the lamp emporium and pick the lock of the main entrance
- They find some imps on the ground floor painting a sign


## Story

The story starts in the mid-sized trade town of [Norville](../../setting/estharia/norville). It's a wet, cold, stinky, rainy spring day, some time between 2 and 4 pm, maybe 3 pm, who's to say, live your own truth or whatever. Four adventurers are coincidentally drawn to Norville for one reason or another.

[Elewyn](../../characters/main-characters/elewyn), the mysterious druid (NOT cleric, thank you very much) was drawn to Norville by the wind, which told her that a beautiful white horse had an injury and needed her help. She found the horse behind the local tavern called [The Lute & Lyre](../../setting/estharia/norville#the-lute--lyre), lying in a stable, injured. Drawing upon her earthly magic, she healed the horse, who gave her a nod and a wink as if to say, "thanks bro, good lookin' out".

[Vesper Treadway](../../characters/main-characters/vesper-treadway) arrived at 3:00 pm as is her own truth and entered the Lute & Lyre. She's been here before, and she'll be back again. She begins to set up to perform, but has arrived a bit early. The tavern is dead, save for a few poor souls already drunk. The coffers are clearly running dry among the patrons here. Even still, the day is early, and she hopes that later on, more people start filing in.

[Aelyra Caladwen](../../characters/main-characters/aelyra-caladwen) and [Syren Ashryver](../../characters/main-characters/syren-ashryver) arrive shortly after Vesper, with the specific intent of Aelyra performing. Aelyra immediately takes note of the stage AND the person on it and immediately thinks, "Oh, her and I could play together, cool!" Aelyra approaches Vesper, who is preparing the space for her performance, and says, "Hey, I see you got an instrument. Yeah. I also have an instrument. Where are you gonna play that instrument? I was gonna do a little ditty." She gestures towareds Syren. "This is my friend. She doesn't have instruments, but she's cool. She's here... She's here for the vibes, okay? And I need money, too."

Syren injerjects on Aelyra's behalf while Aelyra tunes her lyre (a.k.a. baby harp). She invoked Aelyra's status as high nobility of a neighboring country, but Vesper is unimpressed by the credentials. Aelyra begins playing a beautiful quiet ballad-type of song, invoking tears in the eyes of the drunk men all around. Vesper watches, largely unimpressed though silently acknowledging some amount of talent there, straps on her Lute and begins playing Bon Jovi's "Livin' on a Prayer", which also invokes tears. Vesper agrees to allow Aelyra to play as backup, but must follow Vesper's lead. Aelyra agrees, reluctantly.

The characters spend the next few hours "getting hammer drunk" and waiting for more patrons to file in before performing. Hours later, when the tavern finally appears to be sufficiently populated, the musicians take the stage and begin playing what may or may not have been Bob Marley's "Jammin'", and the patrons love it. They're eating it up.

Mere moments into the performance, a man bursts through the tavern door in a panic, shouting for help in a panic. Vesper vaguely recognizes the man as the owner of the [Lamp Emporium](../../setting/estharia/norville#findlays-lamp-emporium), and she and Aelyra stop playing to listen to what he has to say. He notices that he has the attention of the people nearby and continues, rubbing his cheeks with his palms nervously:

> I've spilled tea all over my demonic contracts at the at the lamp shop and now the demon and all the imps that I had serving me are freed and I don't know what to do. They rebelled and kicked me out of the lamp emporium. I can't get back in. They're running the place, Oh, I need to get back in there, I have important blueprints and my spellbook, they have everything. Belvedere, my demon servant, took my finger and is using it to bind a ward that keeps me from being able to enter! Are any of you adventureres that can help me? I can offer many rewards!

 Aelyra immediately agrees to help, and as a result of that, Syren also agrees to help, but only to help Aelyra and keep her out of trouble. Elewyn, who has been silently observing from the corner, emerges and also agrees to assist, insisting that she wants to ensure that nobody gets hurt. Vesper expresses some reluctance, and asks, "Well, what's in it for me?" 

 Findlay lays out his terms. The Imps must be dealt with, and for every imp tail brought to him, he will pay four gold. For one of Belvedere's horns, he will also pay, and he will also pay if Belvedere is demonstrably killed or banished from the natural plane. He will pay for the return of his finger, for the breaking of the ward, for the discovery and return of his spellbook, and for a blueprint for a special lamp that he is working on. Elewyn asks, "Can I have a lamp?" to which Findlay agrees unconditionally. Vesper decides to join, but asks Findlay to watch her lute, to which he also agrees.

 The players follow Findlay back to the Lamp Emporium. Findlay points to a mark on the ground and says, "I can't get any closer than that to the building." The players test the ward, ensuring that they are not also kept out by the magic. They are not, and so Vesper approaches the primary entrance which has a poorly written and heavily mispelled sign that says, "Closed. Reopening under new management. -B". She tries the door, but finds it locked, and so she produces her thieves kit and picks the lock, which is a little more complex than she's accustomed to, but she gets it unlocked without getting anyone's attention and takes a peek inside to scope out the situation.
